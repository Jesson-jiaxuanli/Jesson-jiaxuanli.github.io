# NEFU Outward Bound Faculty Team | 东北林业大学素质拓展讲师团

> 15th Generation Lecturer (第十五代讲师), Nov 2022 – Present
> 第十五代讲师

## Overview

The NEFU Outward Bound Faculty Team (NEFU OBFT), established in 2006, is one of the university's most prestigious student organizations. The team's motto — *"Hone the Will, Build the Team, Unleash Potential, Transcend the Self"* — reflects its mission to deliver experiential education and team-building programs. The selection process is extremely competitive: from hundreds of annual applicants, only about ten are chosen through preliminary interviews, follow-up interviews, and six months of internal training and assessment.

## My Journey

### Selection & Training (Nov 2022 – May 2023)
- Selected as one of the **15th generation lecturers** from hundreds of applicants
- Completed six months of intensive internal training covering:
  - Experiential education theory and methodology
  - Group dynamics and facilitation techniques
  - Risk management and outdoor safety protocols
  - Activity design and curriculum development
- Participated in assessment sessions as both facilitator and participant

### Leadership Role (2023 – 2024)
As a 15th generation lecturer, I took on mentorship responsibilities:
- **Recruited and trained** the 16th and 17th generation lecturers
- **Independently designed** training programs and team-building curricula
- **Led recruitment** processes including screening and assessment

## Activities & Impact

I designed and led approximately **20 team-building activities** across various university groups:

### Groups Served
- Youth Marxist Class (青马班)
- Postgraduate Teaching Support Group (研究生支教团)
- Marxism School postgraduates
- School of Life Sciences freshmen
- Museum volunteer teams
- Various student organizations

### Cumulative Impact
- **Nearly 1,000 participants** trained through our programs
- Programs designed to develop leadership, communication, trust, and collaborative problem-solving skills

## Key Competencies Developed

- **Facilitation skills** — Guiding group activities, managing dynamics, and drawing out learning points
- **Activity design** — Creating structured experiences that balance challenge with support
- **Risk assessment** — Ensuring physical and psychological safety in experiential activities
- **Mentorship** — Training and developing new lecturers, passing on institutional knowledge
- **Public speaking** — Leading large groups through energizers, debriefs, and presentations

## Reflection

My experience as an Outward Bound lecturer fundamentally shaped how I understand team dynamics and experiential learning. The rigorous selection process taught me resilience; the mentorship role taught me patience and teaching; and the 20+ activities I led gave me confidence in facilitation and public engagement. These skills directly transfer to my research career — particularly in cross-cultural collaboration, presenting complex information to diverse audiences, and building collaborative team environments.
