# NEFU Wetland Conservation Association | 东北林业大学湿地保护协会

> Core Member (核心成员), Sep 2022 – Oct 2023
> 核心成员

## Overview

The NEFU Wetland Conservation Association (NEFU WCA) is a student-led organization dedicated to wetland biodiversity research, conservation advocacy, and public environmental education. As a core member, I contributed to scientific research, educational programming, and international communication initiatives that advanced the association's mission.

## Key Contributions

### 🔬 Scientific Research
- **Red Book of Species in Xingkai Lake** — Participated in the compilation of a comprehensive species inventory for the Xingkai Lake region, documenting biodiversity status and conservation priorities

### 🏫 Environmental Education
- **Touyan Nature Education** — Assisted in organizing the "Head Wildfowl Nature Education" program at the Naolihe Nature Reserve, designing hands-on learning activities for local students
- **Campus Wetland Awareness** — Organized a "Wetland Protection Law" awareness campaign on campus, educating students on wetland ecology and legal frameworks

### 📚 Publication & Recognition
- **Bird Guide Project** — Led the proposal and completion of the NEFU Bird Guide精品社团项目 (Quality Club Project), creating a comprehensive visual guide to campus and regional bird species
- **Campus Birdwatching Activity** — Designed and organized a campus bird identification scavenger hunt, which was reported by the *Beidahuang Daily* (北大荒日报)

### 🌐 International Communication
- **Russia-China Student Exchange** — Organized English-language student exchange sessions with the Russian People's Friendship University, fostering international cooperation on conservation topics
- **Association Operations** — Established the association's WeChat public account and managed daily operations, growing the membership base to over 100 students through recruitment campaigns

## Reflection

My time at the NEFU Wetland Conservation Association bridged the gap between academic research and public engagement. The Red Book project taught me the importance of systematic biodiversity documentation, while the nature education activities showed me the power of hands-on learning in fostering environmental awareness. The Beidahuang Daily feature validated our work's impact beyond the university, and the international exchange broadened my perspective on global conservation collaboration. This experience laid the foundation for my later research in ecology and my commitment to science communication.
