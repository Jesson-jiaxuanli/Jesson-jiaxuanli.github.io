Student Rights & Liaison Department, NEFU | 东北林业大学学生会权益联络部

Department Member → Department Head (2020.9 – 2023.6)
部员 → 部长

Overview

I served in the Student Rights & Liaison Department (formerly known as the Discipline Inspection Department) of the College Student Union at Northeast Forestry University for three years, progressing from a regular member to department head. This experience gave me extensive practical training in organizational management, institutional rule enforcement, and sensitive interpersonal communication.

Key Responsibilities

As Department Member (2020.9 – 2022.6)

  • Dormitory inspection — Regularly checked dormitory compliance with electrical appliance regulations and overnight attendance
  • Event management — Maintained order at college events and assisted with ceremonial arrangements
  • Reporting — Prepared weekly reports and monthly summaries on inspection results

As Department Head (2022.9 – 2023.6)

  • Team leadership — Organized weekly departmental meetings, assigned tasks, and mentored junior members
  • Event planning — Designed and executed campus-wide Safety Knowledge Competition and Dormitory Culture Festival for the entire college
  • Sensitive case handling — Managed discipline cases involving dormitory violations, balancing rule enforcement with empathetic communication
  • Cross-department coordination — Collaborated with other student union departments on college-wide initiatives

Key Achievements

  • Safety Knowledge Competition — Designed and organized a college-wide event on fire safety, electrical safety, and emergency response
  • Dormitory Culture Festival — Created a cultural event promoting harmonious dormitory life and community building
  • Systematic reporting — Established standardized weekly and monthly reporting templates adopted by the college

Skills Gained

  • Rule enforcement with empathy — Learned to apply institutional rules fairly while maintaining respect for individuals’ dignity
  • Communication under pressure — Handled sensitive situations requiring negotiation between students and administration
  • Team management — Trained and mentored 10+ junior department members
  • Event planning — Organized large-scale college events from conception to execution

Reflection

This three-year journey through the Student Union taught me that effective organizational management is not just about following rules — it’s about building trust and maintaining communication channels between different stakeholders. As department head, I learned to balance institutional accountability with student welfare, which gave me valuable insights into leadership and conflict resolution that I continue to apply in my academic and research work.
