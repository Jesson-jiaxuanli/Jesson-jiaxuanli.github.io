Bird Rehabilitation Internship — Ulungur Lake, Xinjiang | 新疆福海县乌伦古湖鸟类救治繁育站实习

Intern at the National Wetland Park Administration (Jun – Jul 2023)
乌伦古湖国家级湿地公园管理局实习

Overview

I completed a one-month internship at the Ulanur Lake (乌伦古湖) National Wetland Park Administration’s Bird Rehabilitation and Breeding Station in Fuyun County, Xinjiang. This experience provided comprehensive field ecology training — from avian monitoring to wildlife rehabilitation.

Field Work

Bird Monitoring & Census

  • Conducted systematic bird surveys in the Xiao Haizi (小海子) region of Ulanur Lake
  • Used binoculars and spotting scopes to identify bird species and record population counts
  • Documented habitat distribution and behavioral patterns of waterbirds
  • Species observed included Ruddy Shelduck (赤嘴潜鸭), various grebes, and shorebirds

Wildlife Observations

  • Recorded amphibian and rodent species in the wetland ecosystem
  • Learned species identification techniques for regional fauna
  • Contributed to biodiversity baseline surveys for the wetland park

Specimen & Exhibition Study

  • Studied preserved specimens and exhibition materials at the station
  • Gained comprehensive knowledge of local biodiversity and ecosystem history

Plant Survey

  • Joined a Xinjiang University botany professor on an invasive plant survey
  • Improved plant identification skills through fieldwork in diverse habitats
  • Documented invasive species distribution patterns in the region

Wildlife Rehabilitation

  • Learned bird rehabilitation techniques and first-aid protocols
  • Assisted in the rescue and treatment of an injured Ruddy Shelduck (赤嘴潜鸭)
  • The bird recovered successfully and was returned to health

Key Photos

Skills Gained

  • Avian field techniques — Species ID, point counts, behavioral observation
  • Wildlife rehabilitation — First aid, handling, recovery protocols
  • Botanical survey — Invasive species identification
  • Field data recording — Standardized monitoring protocols
  • Adaptability — Working in remote, arid field conditions

Reflection

The Ulanur Lake internship was my first extended field ecology experience. It taught me that good fieldwork requires patience, systematic observation, and the ability to adapt to challenging conditions. The rehabilitation of the injured Ruddy Shelduck was particularly rewarding — it demonstrated how hands-on conservation work creates direct positive impact on individual animals and their populations.
